Frequently Asked Questions

Carbon credits are calculated based on the carbon savings of your chosen transport method compared to driving alone in a gasoline car. Each transport method has a specific savings rate (in kg CO₂ per km), which is multiplied by your commute distance.

For example, cycling saves approximately 0.192 kg of CO₂ per kilometer compared to driving. So a 5 km bicycle commute would earn you 0.96 carbon credits (0.192 × 5).

Employers benefit in multiple ways:

  • Track and report on organizational sustainability metrics for ESG compliance
  • Accumulate carbon credits that can be traded with other organizations
  • Encourage employee participation in sustainable commuting
  • Enhance corporate sustainability reputation and environmental impact
  • Monitor and report progress toward corporate carbon reduction goals

Our platform includes multiple verification mechanisms:

  • Location data verification when available
  • Employer oversight and approval processes
  • Pattern analysis to detect unusual commuting claims
  • Random verification checks for high-volume users

These measures ensure the integrity of the carbon credit system and maintain trust in the platform.

When carbon credits are traded between organizations, they maintain their environmental value. Organizations can:

  • Apply them toward sustainability reporting and carbon reduction goals
  • Bundle them for certification by environmental standards bodies
  • Convert them to financial benefits through our partner network
  • Donate them to environmental projects and initiatives

All transactions are recorded on our platform for transparency and auditability.
