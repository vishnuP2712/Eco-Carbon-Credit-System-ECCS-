How Carbon Credit Card Works

Our innovative platform tracks, rewards, and facilitates the trading of carbon credits, creating a sustainable ecosystem that benefits both individuals and organizations.

Understanding Carbon Credits

Carbon credits represent reductions in greenhouse gas emissions. By choosing sustainable transportation options like cycling, walking, or public transit, users earn carbon credits that can be accumulated, tracked, and traded within our system.

Our platform incentivizes eco-friendly behaviors by converting carbon-saving actions into valuable credits that can be used for various rewards and benefits.

Why Carbon Credits Matter

  • Provides measurable environmental impact metrics
  • Creates financial incentives for sustainable choices
  • Enables organizations to meet and exceed carbon reduction goals
  • Connects individual actions to global environmental outcomes

The Carbon Credit Process

1

Log Your Commute

Employees track their daily commutes through our user-friendly interface. By providing transport method, start and end locations, and distance traveled, our system calculates the carbon savings compared to a standard gasoline vehicle commute.

Our algorithm uses the following carbon savings rates (kg CO₂ per km):

  • Bicycle: 0.192 kg/km
  • Walking: 0.192 kg/km
  • Bus: 0.103 kg/km
  • Train: 0.120 kg/km
  • Carpool: 0.096 kg/km
  • Electric Car: 0.072 kg/km
  • Motorcycle: 0.061 kg/km
  • Work from Home: 0.192 kg/km
2

Earn Carbon Credits

Once a commute is logged, our system automatically calculates the carbon saved based on the transport method and distance. These savings are immediately converted to carbon credits in your account.

The formula is simple:

Carbon Credits = Savings Rate × Distance (km)

For example, a 10 km bicycle commute would earn:

0.192 kg/km × 10 km = 1.92 carbon credits
3

Track Your Impact

Our comprehensive dashboard provides visual analytics of your carbon savings. View your impact by transport method, track your carbon credit balance, and see your contribution to environmental goals.

Features include:

  • Historical commute data and carbon savings
  • Visual charts breaking down savings by transport method
  • Complete transaction history
  • Progress toward personal and organizational sustainability targets
4

Trade & Utilize Credits

Carbon credits have real value in our ecosystem. Employers can collect credits from their employees and trade them with other organizations. This creates a marketplace for carbon offsets that rewards sustainable practices.

Credits can be used for:

  • Meeting corporate sustainability goals
  • Trading with other organizations
  • Redeeming for rewards and benefits
  • Contributing to certified environmental projects
